What is this?

The Witness Protocol · an open standard for computable trust · back to the chain

The problem

Almost everything you do online runs on hope. You hope the seller is real. You hope the reviewer isn't paid. You hope the "support" email isn't a scam. You hope the AI you're talking to answers to somebody. There's infrastructure for moving money and infrastructure for moving messages — but no infrastructure for trust. You just guess, and sometimes you get burned, and the person who burned you carries nothing forward. Their next victim starts from hope too.

The idea

Trust is consistency, compounded by time. A rate, not a state.

That's how trust actually works between people — you trust your mate because he's shown up reliably for twenty years, not because he says he's reliable. This protocol makes that measurable. Any entity — a person, an AI agent, a device, an organisation — gets an identity it alone controls, and its actions get witnessed: signed statements from others, chained into a permanent public log. From that record, anyone can compute a trust rate. It grows slowly with consistent behaviour, craters when trust is broken, rebuilds slower than it fell, and decays if you disappear. Nobody assigns it. Nobody can edit it. It's arithmetic on evidence.

What keeps it honest

Accusations are claims, not verdicts. A claim of broken trust costs nothing for seven days, during which the accused can dispute it — and a disputed claim carries half weight, with the disagreement itself recorded forever on both parties' histories. You can't buy tenure, you can't farm the score with fake volume, you can't delete your past, and lying as a witness stakes your own rate. The rules bind everyone identically, including the people who built this — the first entity ever constrained by this protocol was its own founder.

If you just joined

Your device created its own cryptographic key — it never left your hands — and registered on the chain. You're early: the network is days old, and honestly, the benefit to you today is small. What you own is a place in the genesis of the thing and an identity whose trust can only be earned the slow way — which is the only way that means anything.

The bones

Open protocol, open source, royalty-free patent pledge (AU provisional 2026906417). Not a blockchain, not a token, nothing for sale. Three operations — register · witness · verify — and one idea old as a handshake, finally countable.
